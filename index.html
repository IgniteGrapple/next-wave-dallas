The label

Hip hop isn’t the problem. It’s the path out.

Next Wave Dallas is a hip hop / trap-specific nonprofit label. Not a traditional label, not just a social service org—both, under one roof.

Read mission & vision

Mission

Empower the next wave of hip hop artists through healing, housing, and higher education—without violence, without exploitation.

Vision

A world where trap and R&B aren’t tools of survival, but platforms for healing, ownership, and lasting change—starting in Dallas.

Core values

  • Anti-violence & pro-autonomy
  • Artists retain ownership of their work
  • Trauma-informed, justice-focused
  • No religious angle—fully secular

We invest in young artists the system left behind and help them build something real: a catalog they own, degrees that travel with them, and a support system that doesn’t disappear when the project drops.

Programs

Two lanes into the same family.

In-house housing for the artists who need it most. Outpatient support for those already in the community. Same expectations: renounce violence, show up, do the work.

See full program model

In-house (residential)

  • Transitional / supportive housing in Dallas
  • Mandatory renouncement of violence & drill crews
  • Therapy, MRT, anger management, psychiatry
  • Case management by full-time social worker

Outpatient (community)

  • Outreach social worker for justice-impacted artists
  • Support around probation, court, employment
  • Help preparing for shows, releases, interviews
  • Pathway to earn housing through progress

Education pathway

Dallas College partnership in progress: GED support plus Commercial Music A.A.S. tracks in Music Business, Performing, Producing / Songwriting, and Recording Technology.

Residential artists live on-site under a harm-reduction housing model aligned with probation conditions. Outpatient artists check in regularly while still living in the community. Both groups work toward music, mental health, education, and employment milestones that are tracked for courts, donors, and the artists themselves.

For artists

Who we’re built for.

Trap and R&B artists with real stories who are ready to leave violence behind and build careers with support, not exploitation.

How selection works

You might be a fit if…

  • You make hip hop / trap / R&B with something to say.
  • You’re navigating court, probation, or unstable housing.
  • You’re open to therapy, coaching, and honest feedback.

What you get

  • Access to respectful studios, producers & engineers.
  • Case management, mental health support, and housing pathways.
  • Education options through GED & Commercial Music degrees.
  • Shows, press, and brand opportunities aligned with your story.

Pilot cohort

Year one: 3–5 Dallas artists. A small roster, hands-on support, and a collaborative label project (EP or mixtape) that doubles as a fundraiser.

Artists are referred by courts, probation officers, schools, community orgs—and by the streets themselves. Every artist goes through a screening process focused on safety, readiness for change, and willingness to work in a nonviolent space.

Partners & donors

A label that courts, funders and studios can trust.

We sit at the table with probation, public defenders, mental health orgs, and music industry partners—so everyone’s pulling in the same direction.

Partnership menu

Courts & probation

  • Alternative-to-incarceration referrals
  • Clear reporting on program milestones
  • Secure, staffed housing for select clients

Funders & donors

  • Underwrite beds, therapy, and tuition
  • Support staff: social workers, security, admin, legal
  • Back the first cohort and inaugural label project

Industry & brands

  • Studios, producers, engineers offering fair deals
  • Venues for sober showcases & community dinners
  • Publicists and branding mentors who center empowerment

We’re designing a funding model that blends donations, grants, a modest royalty cap (up to 5% back to the org), and merch / performance revenue—so the label is sustainable without ever robbing the artists.
