Label overview
Mission & Vision

Mission: Empowering the next wave of hip hop artists through healing, housing, and higher education—without violence, without exploitation.

Next Wave Dallas is a hip hop/trap–specific nonprofit record label. We invest in young artists the system left behind and help them build something real. We replace survival cycles with creative freedom, ownership, and opportunity—through trap and R&B music that uplifts instead of destroys.

What makes us different

  • Hip hop isn’t the problem. We treat it as the path out—not something to blame.
  • Nonviolent, anti-exploitation model. Artists renounce violence and get support to build something better.
  • Ownership first. Artists retain ownership of their work. The label takes at most a 5% royalty to keep the nonprofit sustainable.
  • Secular & trauma-informed. No religious angle. Support is rooted in criminology, social work, and harm reduction.

Vision

A world where trap and R&B aren’t tools of survival—but platforms for healing, ownership, and lasting change.

  • Dallas becomes a national model for nonviolent, artist-centered hip hop work.
  • Our blueprint can be replicated in other cities and labels.
  • Artists leave with credits, degrees, keys to housing, and healthier relationships.
Programming model
In-House & Outpatient Programs

Our programming model is simple on purpose: two primary paths, same expectations, different levels of structure.

In-House Program Clients (Residential)

For artists living in our transitional or supportive housing.

  • Mandatory renouncement of violence or drill crew activity.
  • Mandatory counseling and treatment (anger management, MRT, therapy, psychiatry).
  • Full-time in-house social worker provides case management and crisis support.
  • Housing rules aligned with probation or court conditions when applicable.

Education Partnership – Dallas College

Paige and Kevin will pursue a partnership with Dallas College to connect artists to GED and Commercial Music A.A.S. programs, including:

  • Music Business & Entrepreneurship A.A.S. – approx. $5,940 for 2 years
  • Performing Musician A.A.S. – approx. $5,940 for 2 years
  • Producer & Songwriter A.A.S. – approx. $5,940 for 2 years
  • Recording Technology A.A.S. – approx. $5,940 for 2 years

Part of the partnership conversation will include FAFSA support and tailored schedules for justice-impacted students.

Outpatient Program Clients (Non-Residential)

For artists who don’t live in the house but still want support and accountability.

  • Led by an outreach social worker who moves between courts, jails, studios, and the community.
  • Same values, lighter contact hours and check-ins.
  • Help with employment prep, transportation, appointments, and crisis response.
  • Artists can earn access to housing through progress and participation.

Housing & healing
Social Workers, Casework & Safety

Inpatient Social Worker – Supportive Housing Case Manager

Role: Primary point of contact for residents living in on-site or long-term transitional housing.

  • Conducts needs assessments and builds individualized support plans.
  • Coordinates with legal teams, probation, healthcare, and community resources.
  • Tracks progress toward employment, sobriety, mental health, family reunification, and independent living.
  • Helps clients understand and comply with housing rules and probation conditions.
  • Maintains documentation, incident reports, and progress notes.
  • Collaborates with security and leadership to respond to crises in real time.

Outreach Case Manager – Outpatient Social Worker

Role: Equal parts advocate, case manager, and connector for non-residential clients.

  • Conducts outreach in courts, jails, the community, and online.
  • Builds trust-based relationships and helps clients set short- and long-term goals.
  • Provides case management: employment prep, transportation, appointments, crisis response.
  • Helps clients navigate probation terms, court conditions, and bureaucratic systems.
  • Supports creative goals—shows, interviews, grants, gigs, collaborations.

Safety & Protection Officer (Security Guard)

Role: Ensures the safety of clients and staff with a trauma-informed, dignified approach.

  • Monitors facility entrances, exits, and common areas.
  • Supports staff with conflict de-escalation.
  • Coordinates with local law enforcement only when appropriate.
  • Maintains incident logs and safety reports.

Administrative & Legal Support Coordinator

  • Greets visitors, answers phones, and manages scheduling.
  • Maintains case files, internal databases, and intake paperwork.
  • Supports communication with probation officers, public defenders, and partners.
  • Organizes internal meetings, agendas, and minutes.
For artists
Who we’re looking for & what you can expect

Next Wave Dallas is for trap and R&B artists who are ready to leave violence behind and build a career with ownership and support.

Who you are

  • You have real talent and real life on you—maybe probation, court dates, or unstable housing.
  • You’re willing to renounce violence and drill crew activity.
  • You’re open to therapy, casework, and honest conversations.
  • You want to own your masters and build something long-term.

What we bring

  • Access to studios, producers, engineers, and creative collaborators.
  • Case management, housing pathways, and mental health support.
  • Education pathways into GED and Commercial Music programs.
  • Shows, press, and brand opportunities aligned with your story—not exploitation.

Year 1 focus

  • Pilot cohort of 3–5 artists with individualized plans.
  • A collaborative project (mixtape/EP) as fundraiser and showcase.
  • Tracking impact beyond streams: housing stability, education, income, and wellbeing.
For partners & donors
How courts, funders & industry allies plug in

Next Wave Dallas runs on a mix of donations, grants, ethical royalties (max 5%), merch, and performances. We’re looking for partners who share our values.

Court & probation partners

  • Refer eligible clients to in-house or outpatient programs.
  • Collaborate on alternatives to incarceration rooted in music and education.

Funders & donors

  • Underwrite housing units, therapy hours, and tuition for Commercial Music degrees.
  • Support staff positions: social workers, safety officer, administrative coordinator.

Industry partners

  • Studios, producers, and engineers offering ethical deals and mentorship.
  • Venues for sober showcases, community dinners, and web-series tapings.
  • Publicists and branding mentors aligned with empowerment, not exploitation.
