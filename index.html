Mission & Vision

Why Next Wave Dallas exists.

We are not “just another music company.” We are a criminology-informed, trauma-informed response to how the industry and the streets have exploited hip hop talent for decades.

Hip hop and trap are not the problem—they’re the way out. We treat the art and the artist with the same level of care.

Mission

One sentence mission, real-world impact.

Mission: Empowering the next wave of hip hop artists through healing, housing, and higher education—without violence, without exploitation.

We replace cycles of survival with creative freedom, ownership, and opportunity—through trap and R&B music that uplifts instead of destroys.

Vision

What we’re building toward.

Vision: A world where trap and R&B aren’t tools of survival—but platforms for healing, ownership, and lasting change.

Our long game is a blueprint that can be replicated in other cities: housing connected to studios, social work connected to artist development, policy change connected to what happens in the booth.

Programming Model

How artists move through the Next Wave.

Two main lanes: In-house residential and Outpatient non-residential. Same standards. Different levels of structure and contact.

Every path is rooted in renouncing violence, committing to healing, and building a real future in music and education.

In-House Program (Residential)
Transitional or supportive housing for artists committed to deep change.
Highest structure
  • Residents live in program housing with an onsite social worker.
  • Mandatory renouncement of violence / drill crew activity and any content that glorifies harm.
  • Mandatory counseling, anger management, therapy & psychiatry as needed.
  • Case management focused on employment, sobriety & stability.
  • Clear pathways into GED or A.A.S. programs at Dallas College:
    • Music Business & Entrepreneurship A.A.S.
    • Performing Musician A.A.S.
    • Producer & Songwriter A.A.S.
    • Recording Technology A.A.S.
    • Each projected at ≈ $5,940 / 2 years (with FAFSA & aid support).
Outpatient Program (Non-residential)
For artists who aren’t in housing but are actively engaged in the work.
Flexible structure
  • Led by the Outpatient Social Worker / Outreach Case Manager.
  • Same culture and expectations, with lighter contact hours.
  • Focus on reentry support, stability, and creative milestones.
  • Access to studio time, shows, groups & referrals.
  • Clear pathway: progress can unlock access to housing spots.

Goals, Content & Community

What Year 1 looks like in real life.

Before we scale, we prove the model. That means a small cohort of artists, a house that runs well, and a story the city can actually see.

Think: fewer artists, deeper impact. Less hype, more receipts. We’re building something that can stand up to scrutiny.

Early goals & pilot planning.
  • Artist cohort: 3–5 artists in Year 1 for deep work vs. shallow reach.
  • Secure the house: Identify & lock in a property suitable for transitional housing, studio build-out & small group programming.
  • First project: A nonprofit mixtape or EP that doubles as a fundraiser and a showcase of healing-centered trap & R&B.
  • Data & impact: Track outcomes in housing stability, recidivism, education enrollment, and creative milestones.
  • Board formation: Build a founding board with lived experience, legal/ mental health expertise, and community trust.
Content & community presence.
  • Instagram, Threads & YouTube documenting real transformation.
  • Web series following artists from courtrooms and couches to studios and stages.
  • Quarterly showcases, listening sessions & community dinners.
  • Behind-the-scenes storytelling around housing, healing & higher ed wins.

The goal: let Dallas—and future funders—watch the journey in real time, without exploiting anyone’s trauma for views.

Timeline

From idea to open doors.

Exact dates will shift as funding & partnerships lock in, but this is the arc from concept to a fully operating pilot by Fall 2026.

Register the org, secure the house, hire the core team, enroll first artists—then refine and scale what works.

Phase 1
Legal formation & blueprint.
Incorporate as a nonprofit, begin 501(c)(3) application, formalize mission, vision, programming model, and board recruitment. Clarify the 5% max royalty clause and ownership structures.
Phase 2
Partnerships & fundraising.
Lock in Dallas College partnership, mental health collaborators, legal partners, and initial publicists/branding mentors. Begin grant applications, donor pitches, and crowdfunding anchored around the pilot.
Phase 3
Housing & hiring.
Secure the house or facility. Hire Inpatient & Outpatient Social Workers, Safety & Protection Officer, Administrative & Legal Support Coordinator, and Head of Artist Development & Public Voice.
Phase 4
Pilot launch – Fall 2026 target.
Launch a 3–5 artist pilot cohort. Begin in-house and outpatient services, GED/A.A.S. enrollment, and studio work. Document the process via web series & events.
Phase 5
Evaluation & expansion.
Evaluate outcomes, refine programming, and build a funding case to scale: more beds, more artists, and eventually more cities running their own “Next Wave” chapters.
