The label

Hip hop isn’t the problem—it’s the path out.

Next Wave Dallas is a hip hop / trap–specific nonprofit record label. We combine artist development with housing, mental health, and higher education in one ecosystem.

Mission

Empower the next wave of hip hop artists through healing, housing, and higher education— without violence and without exploitation.


Vision

A world where trap and R&B aren’t tools of survival, but platforms for healing, ownership, and lasting change starting in Dallas.

Core principles
  • Anti-violence and pro-autonomy.
  • Artists retain ownership of their work.
  • Trauma-informed care rooted in criminology.
  • No religious angle—fully secular support.
Programs

Two ways into the family.

In-house housing for artists who need stability. Outpatient support for artists already in the community. Same expectations: renounce violence and do the work.

In-house (residential)
  • Transitional / supportive housing in Dallas.
  • Mandatory renouncement of violence and drill crew activity.
  • Therapy, anger management, MRT, psychiatry.
  • Full-time social worker for case management.
Outpatient (non-residential)
  • Outreach social worker who meets artists where they are.
  • Support around probation terms, court dates and employment.
  • Help with shows, releases, interviews and grants.
  • Pathway to earn housing through progress and consistency.

Education: Dallas College partnership (in progress)
  • GED support for artists who need a diploma.
  • Commercial Music A.A.S. tracks: Music Business, Performing Musician, Producer & Songwriter, Recording Technology.
For artists

Who we’re built for.

Trap and R&B artists and producers with real stories, who are ready to leave violence behind and build careers with support—not exploitation.

You might be a fit if…
  • You create hip hop / trap / R&B and take your craft seriously.
  • You’re navigating probation, court, or unstable housing.
  • You’re willing to renounce violence and work in a non-violent space.
  • You’re open to therapy, coaching and honest feedback.
What you get
  • Studios, producers and engineers who respect your story.
  • Case management, mental health support and housing pathways.
  • Education options through GED and Commercial Music degrees.
  • Shows, press and public-voice support aligned with your brand.

Year one: a pilot cohort of 3–5 artists in Dallas, building a collaborative project (EP or mixtape) that doubles as a fundraiser and proof of concept.

Partners

Courts, funders and industry at the same table.

Next Wave Dallas is built so probation, public defenders, foundations and music partners can plug in without compromising safety or artist ownership.

Courts & probation
  • Alternative-to-incarceration and re-entry placements.
  • Clear reporting on attendance, milestones and progress.
  • Secure, staffed housing for select clients.
Funders & industry
  • Underwrite beds, therapy hours and tuition.
  • Support key roles: social workers, lawyer, security, admin.
  • Studios, producers, venues and publicists who respect artist autonomy.

The long-term funding model blends donations, grants, a modest royalty cap (up to 5% back to the org), merch and performances—so the label is sustainable without ever robbing the artists.
