About the label

Hip hop is not the problem. It’s the blueprint out.

Next Wave Dallas is a hip hop/trap–focused nonprofit rooted in criminology and trauma-informed care. We’re here for the artist with a record and a story, not just a follower count. Our mission is to replace survival cycles with creative freedom, ownership, and opportunity.

Mission: Empowering the next wave of hip hop artists through healing, housing, and higher education—without violence, without exploitation.

Mission
Why this label exists.

The streets, the system, and the industry have all eaten off of hip hop artists. We’re building the opposite: a label that protects artists while they grow.

  • Trap & R&B treated as tools for healing, not just survival.
  • Artists supported with housing, case management, and mental health care.
  • Every deal built around ownership and long-term stability.
Vision
Where we’re headed.

A world where trap and R&B are platforms for ownership and lasting change—not soundtracks to violence and exploitation.

  • Dallas as a model for nonviolent, artist-centered hip hop work.
  • A blueprint that can be replicated in other cities and scenes.
  • Artists who leave with credits, degrees, and keys—literally.
Programs

Two lanes into the Next Wave.

We keep the structure simple on purpose: two paths, same expectations. Artists commit to renouncing violence, doing the inner work, and showing up for their craft.

Every artist gets a plan blending music, mental health, housing, and higher education.

In-House Program (Residential) Highest structure

Transitional or supportive housing for artists ready to go all in on healing and building a real future.

  • Live in program housing with a full-time in-house social worker.
  • Mandatory renouncement of violence / drill crew activity.
  • Required counseling, anger management, and therapy where needed.
  • Case management around employment, sobriety, and stability.
  • Pathways into Dallas College programs:
    • Music Business & Entrepreneurship A.A.S.
    • Performing Musician A.A.S.
    • Producer & Songwriter A.A.S.
    • Recording Technology A.A.S.
Outpatient Program (Non-Residential) Flexible structure

For artists who aren’t in the house but want support, accountability, and a serious lane in music.

  • Led by an outreach social worker who meets artists where they are.
  • Same values, lighter contact hours and check-ins.
  • Help with court dates, probation terms, and basic stability.
  • Access to studio time, shows, workshops, and collaborations.
  • Progress can earn access to in-house housing opportunities.
Artists & impact

Fewer artists, deeper work.

We’re not chasing a giant roster. We’re focused on 3–5 serious artists in the pilot that we can walk with from case management and courtrooms to stages and credits.

Think of it as a small label, a small house, and a big net of support around every artist.

Year 1 goals
Pilot cohort: 3–5 artists.

Trap & R&B artists with real talent and real barriers—probation, housing instability, trauma, or all of the above.

  • Artists selected for both skill and readiness for change.
  • Custom plans for music, housing, school, and income.
  • Milestones in studio output, education, and wellness.
First release
Next Wave Vol. 1

A collaborative mixtape or EP functioning as both fundraiser and proof of concept: nonviolent trap and R&B rooted in real stories.

  • Recorded with ethical producers and engineers.
  • Artist-centered splits and transparent contracts.
  • Rollout driven by storytelling, not trauma-bait.
How we measure impact
Beyond streams and views.
  • Housing stability and reduced justice involvement.
  • GED/A.A.S. enrollment, credits earned, and graduation.
  • Income from shows, features, and placements.
  • Self-reported wellbeing and support networks.

Social work
In-House & Outreach Social Workers

One manages the house. One moves through the streets and studios. Together, they keep artists connected and supported.

  • Case management, crisis support, and resource navigation.
  • Help with probation terms, court conditions, and stability.
  • Support artists for shows, interviews, and grants.
Legal & safety
Lawyer & Safety & Protection Officer

A licensed attorney and a safety officer working together so artists can relax, record, and move safely.

  • Draft & review contracts and probation-related legal issues.
  • Keep the org and projects compliant.
  • Maintain a secure, dignified environment at the house and events.
Operations
Administrative & Legal Support Coordinator

The first face people meet and the reason everything runs on time.

  • Manages phones, scheduling, and client intake.
  • Keeps case files, records, and internal databases tight.
  • Coordinates internal meetings, agendas, and logistics.
